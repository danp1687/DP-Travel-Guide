The Auschwitz-Birkenau State Museum

Explore the dark past of Poland

Discover the poignant history of the Auschwitz-Birkenau State Museum in Oświęcim, Poland, a profound destination that invites you to reflect on the resilience and strength of the human spirit. As the most notorious site of the Holocaust, this memorial stands as a testament to the millions of lives lost during one of history's darkest chapters. By visiting, you'll walk through the gates and barracks, see artifacts that tell personal stories, and grasp the depth of human suffering and courage.

Auschwitz-Birkenau was originally built as a concentration camp for Polish political prisoners in 1940, but it quickly expanded to become a major extermination center where over 1.1 million people, mostly Jews, perished. The museum comprises the remains of both the Auschwitz I camp and the Auschwitz II-Birkenau camp, providing visitors with a stark and sobering experience. You can explore the chilling remnants of the gas chambers, crematoria, and the railway tracks that brought countless victims to their fate.

Guided tours offer deep insights into the daily lives of prisoners, the atrocities committed, and the resilience of those who survived. You'll also learn about the courageous efforts of those who resisted and the liberation of the camp by Soviet forces in January 1945. This visit isn't just a history lesson; it's an emotional journey that underscores the importance of memory and the fight against intolerance and injustice. By visiting Auschwitz-Birkenau, you honor the memory of the victims and contribute to the global promise of "never again."

Location: Oświęcim, Poland
